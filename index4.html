Arbroath Smokie
traditional smoked haddock

Ingredients

  • 2 Arbroath smokies
  • 500ml milk
  • 50g butter
  • 50g plain flour
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley
  • Fresh chives
  • Crusty bread to serve

Instructions

  • Place smokies in a pan, cover with milk. Simmer for 5-8 minutes.
  • Remove fish, strain milk, set aside. Flake fish, remove skin and bones.
  • In a separate pan, melt butter, add flour, cook for 1 minute.
  • Gradually whisk in reserved milk until smooth and thickened.
  • Fold in flaked fish and parsley. Serve hot with crusty bread.
🐟 A protected Scottish delicacy. Traditionally smoked over hardwood.
Scotch Eggs
eggs wrapped in sausage meat

Ingredients

  • 6 large eggs
  • 500g sausage meat
  • 100g plain flour
  • 150g breadcrumbs
  • Vegetable oil for deep frying
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  • Boil 4 eggs for 6 minutes, cool in ice water, peel carefully.
  • Divide sausage meat into 4 portions. Flatten each into a patty.
  • Wrap each egg in sausage meat, seal completely.
  • Roll in flour, beaten egg, then breadcrumbs.
  • Heat oil to 170°C. Deep fry for 6-8 minutes until golden.
  • Drain on paper towel. Serve warm or cold.
🥚 Perfect for picnics! Serve with mustard or chutney.
Full Scottish Breakfast
hearty traditional Scottish breakfast

Ingredients

  • 2 Lorne sausages
  • 2 slices black pudding
  • 2 slices bacon
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 tomato, halved
  • 100g mushrooms
  • 2 slices toast or tattie scones
  • Baked beans (optional)

Instructions

  • Heat a large pan. Cook sausages, bacon and black pudding.
  • Add tomatoes and mushrooms, cook until softened.
  • Remove meats, keep warm. Fry eggs in remaining fat.
  • Toast bread or heat tattie scones.
  • Serve everything hot on a large plate.
🏴󠁧󠁢󠁳󠁣󠁴󠁿 The ultimate Scottish breakfast! Serve with strong tea.
